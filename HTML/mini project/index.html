Taylor Swift Discography

Who is Taylor Swift?

Taylor Alison Swift is an American singer-songwriter, multiple Grammy award winner who released 10 albums throughout her career, with genres ranging from country to folk. She is, without a doubt, one of the most succesful and talented artists of the century.

Taylor Swift

Taylor Swift is the debut studio album by American singer-songwriter Taylor Swift, released on October 24, 2006, by Big Machine Records. Swift was 16 years old at the time of the album's release and wrote its songs during her freshman year of high school. Swift has writing credits on all of the album's songs, including those co-written with Liz Rose. Swift experimented with several producers, ultimately choosing Nathan Chapman, who had produced her demo album. Musically, the album is country music styled, and lyrically it speaks of romantic relationships, a couple of which Swift wrote from observing relationships before being in one. Lyrics also touch on Swift's personal struggles in high school.

Fearless

Taylor Swift's second album, "Fearless," released in 2008, marked a pivotal moment in her career and showcased her immense talent as a singer-songwriter. With this album, Swift not only solidified her place as a country-pop crossover sensation but also established herself as a master storyteller. "Fearless" was a deeply personal and relatable collection of songs, where Swift fearlessly explored themes of love, heartbreak, and self-discovery. From the infectious and upbeat anthem "Love Story" to the introspective ballad "White Horse," each track captured a different facet of Swift's journey through adolescence and young adulthood. The album's success was unprecedented, propelling Swift into the mainstream spotlight and earning her numerous accolades, including four Grammy Awards.

Speak Now

Speak Now is the third studio album by American singer-songwriter Taylor Swift. It was released on October 25, 2010, by Big Machine Records. Production for the album took place during 2009 to 2010 at several recording studios, and was handled by Swift and Nathan Chapman. Written entirely by Swift as the follow-up to Fearless, Speak Now expands on the country pop style of her previous work, and features lyrical themes concerning love, romance and heartbreak.

Red

Red is the fourth studio album by American singer-songwriter Taylor Swift. It was released on October 22, 2012, by Big Machine Records. Inspired by past romantic relationships evoking intense, tumultuous "red" emotions that Swift was experiencing during the conception of the project, Red touches on Swift's recurring themes of romance and heartbreak, but represents a more mature perspective and also explores the pressure of being in the limelight. The album features collaborations with producers and guest artists such as Gary Lightbody and Ed Sheeran, and genres such as pop, rock, and country.

1989

1989 is the fifth studio album by American singer-songwriter Taylor Swift, released on October 27, 2014, through Big Machine Records. Following the release of her fourth studio album Red (2012), which critics considered Swift's emergence from her trademark country styles to straightforward pop, Swift sought to completely move away from country on her next record. She enlisted a new set of producers, including Jack Antonoff, Max Martin, Shellback, Ryan Tedder, and Imogen Heap, and shifted her musical style from the country pop of her previous albums to an electropop sound inspired by the music of the 1980s. Lyrically, the album is a concept album based on Swift's romantic relationships and heartbreaks.

Reputation

Released on November 10, 2017, through Big Machine Records. Taylor Swift's sixth studio album, "Reputation," marked a bold departure from her previous musical style and showcased a more mature and edgy version of Swift. The album's overarching theme was Swift's response to the media and public perception of her, allowing her to reclaim her narrative and embrace her imperfections. "Reputation" was a sonic departure for Swift, incorporating elements of electropop, synth-pop, and trap beats, giving the album a darker and more atmospheric sound.

Lover

Lover is the seventh studio album by American singer-songwriter Taylor Swift, released on August 23, 2019, through Republic Records. As the executive producer, Swift enlisted producers Jack Antonoff, Louis Bell, Frank Dukes, and Joel Little for the project. Lover is primarily a pop, synth-pop, electropop, and pop rock record with a retro sensibility, encompassing diverse genres such as country, funk, dream pop, folk, rock and roll, R&B, bubblegum pop, pop punk, and indie pop. Described by Swift as a "love letter to love itself", Lover celebrates the ups and downs of love, incorporating lighter and brighter tones compared to those of its predecessor, Reputation (2017). The album's themes include vulnerability, self-love, and the complexities of relationships.

Folklore

Swift announced the surprise album on July 23, 2020, on social media, less than 24 hours before its release. Taylor Swift's eighth studio album, "folklore," marked a stunning departure from her previous pop-driven sound and introduced a new era of introspection and storytelling. The album was a captivating exploration of indie-folk and alternative soundscapes, where Swift collaborated with acclaimed producer Aaron Dessner of The National, among others. "folklore" offered a departure from the glitz and glamour often associated with Swift's music, instead immersing listeners in a world of introspection, nostalgia, and storytelling.

Evermore

Evermore is the ninth studio album by American singer-songwriter Taylor Swift. It was released on December 11, 2020, through Republic Records, less than five months after Swift's eighth studio album, Folklore (2020). Evermore is a "sister record" to Folklore, both being surprise albums announced less than 24 hours before release. Swift wrote and recorded the album with Aaron Dessner, Jack Antonoff, and Justin Vernon, while collaborating with various other artists. Musically, Evermore continues the indie folk and alternative rock styles of its predecessor, while also incorporating elements of country, dream pop, chamber pop, and folktronica.

Fearless (Taylor's Version)

Fearless (Taylor's Version) is the first re-recorded album by American singer-songwriter Taylor Swift, released on April 9, 2021, through Republic Records. It is a re-recording of Swift's second studio album, Fearless (2008), and the first of six re-recorded albums Swift plans to release, following the ownership dispute of her back catalog. The album was produced by Swift, Christopher Rowe, Jack Antonoff, and Aaron Dessner, and features guest vocals from Keith Urban, Maren Morris, and Colbie Caillat. The album is a faithful re-recording of Fearless, with Swift re-recording all of the album's songs, as well as six additional songs that were written during the same time period but were not included on the original album.

Red (Taylor's Version)

Red (Taylor's Version) is the second re-recorded album by American singer-songwriter Taylor Swift, released on November 12, 2021, through Republic Records. It is a re-recording of Swift's fourth studio album, Red (2012), and the second of six re-recorded albums Swift plans to release, following the ownership dispute of her back catalog. The album is a faithful re-recording of Red, with Swift re-recording all of the album's songs, as well as seven additional songs that were written during the same time period but were not included on the original album.

Midnights

Taylor Swift's tenth studio album, Midnights, was released on October 21, 2022. The album is a concept album about nocturnal ruminations, and was written and produced by Swift with longtime collaborator Jack Antonoff. Inspired by Swift's "sleepless nights", Midnights explores topics of anxiety, insecurity, self-criticism, self-awareness, insomnia, and self-confidence, using confessional yet cryptic lyrics.
